One of the most common questions we get — from both first-time donors and grant reviewers — is the same: how much of my dollar makes it into a classroom?

The honest answer is "almost all of it, and here's the exact path."

## The ledger rules

Every sponsorship and project gift is tagged on entry. Tags map to a board-approved line item; line items map to a school or a program; each school and program has an owner who signs off on disbursements quarterly. Nothing moves without that sign-off, and nothing gets re-tagged after the fact.

## Overhead is visible and capped

Our total operating overhead sits between 6 and 9 percent of gross donations, depending on the year. That covers bank fees, the small software stack we use, the audit, and the one half-time staff role on the U.S. side. Board members are unpaid. The detailed breakdown lives in the annual Form 990 and the yearly transparency report.

## Where the questions usually land

Donors sometimes ask whether a sponsorship can be "saved up" across months. We don't do that — each month's sponsorship covers that month's tuition and meal costs for one student, and un-spent overages get reassigned at the board's quarterly review. If you want a lump-sum gift to be held back for a specific future project, label it clearly on the donate form and we'll honor that instruction.
